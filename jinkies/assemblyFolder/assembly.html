This lightning window is the aggregated work of the whole team. It is filled with tricks and gags that will make a thunder storm experience that people will ne-v-er for-get! The rattling effect, stained glass, rain illusion, and lightning strikes have been improved after receiving feedback in the concept refinement stage. In addition to those, we added a creepy shadow effect to further contribute to a mysterious atmosphere.

A 3D, interactive model of the prototype is accessible above and a slideshow of isolated components can be viewed below. The numbers correspond to entries in the Bill of Materials which is available in the logistics section. For more details, see the individual sections!

  • Overall Dimensions

    A scaled down window was created to demonstrate the concept. The depth should be restricted so that the window can be easily fitted into a wall.


  • Aesthetic and Structure

    A pane of stained glass was fitted into a wood-frame and mounted above a radiator box. Crown moulding and lace curtains were added to further suggest a Victorian aesthetic. The framing behind the window was used for mounting mechanical and electrical components for the gag.


  • Thunder and Lightning

    Thunder sounds from the speaker system were synchronized with flashes of the high-power LEDs to simulate a lightning storm. Parchment paper with layered cut-outs were used to diffuse the light, generate shadows, and provide the illusion of depth.


  • Rain and Wind

    A combination of globular static hot-glue droplets on the window and streaks on a transparent rolling track were used to simulate rain. Rainfall and wind sounds were played through the speaker system.


  • Hand Shadow

    An ultrasound sensor was used to detect the presence of guests. When triggered, a servo-motor swung a hand towards the window to startle the viewer. A corresponding "smack" was also played through the speaker for a more pronounced effect.
