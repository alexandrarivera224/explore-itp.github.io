What are we exploring, and Why?

The Arctic Ocean is a mystery, and what we claim to know about it thus far is based on a sparse amount of data, the knowledge gaps numerous. To make things more difficult, ice covers large portions of the ocean, hindering the use of conventional methods of sampling water properties. To overcome this obstacle in order to collect more information about the Arctic Ocean, a new measuring device called the ‘Ice-tethered profiler’ (ITP) was designed. What’s the big deal, and how does it work? The system’s top portion sits on the surface of the ice, and a long wire rope extends through the ice, down into the water, and through the water column. The component that actually conducts the sampling moves vertically along the wire, collecting data in the form of pressure, temperature, and salinity. This data is then sent in real-time to WHOI via a satellite link, now accessible for exploration and analysis.

Since the first deployments in the early 2000s, a large amount of data has been collected. This data spans the observations of over 100 machines and many are still actively retrieving information. The database is growing continuously, and our understanding of the Arctic Ocean and its secrets right alongside it.

Want to join us?

This exploration was made possible through the collaborative efforts of the Duke Keep Exploring Initiative and Woods Hole Oceanographic Institute. With access to this database, we seek to explore and visualize the data, highlighting trends of these physical properties over space and time. We weave a narrative of the Arctic’s waters that will engage you through a creative, yet scientifically-sound, interpretation of the data at hand. We hope that you join us on this exploration, beginning at the water’s surface and making a gradual descent, sharing insights as we go.

Will you join us?

Navigate your way...

...by using the panel located underneath the title. You can explore the data through space and explore the data through time. As you go, we share our process, methodology and motives.
